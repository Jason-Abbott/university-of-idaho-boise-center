Fall Calendar
Sept 15
12:10 to 12:50 p.m.		 Money 2000 Informational meeting. Ada County Bldg., 650 W. Main, Boise, 3rd floor hearing room. Bring a sack lunch! Money 2000 helps families and individuals increase savings/investment and reduce debt. You’ll learn how it works & you’ll gain some money saving tips. No charge for this meeting. Preregister at 377-2107.
Sept 16
10:00 to 11:00 a.m.		 Preventing Food Poisoning. Meridian Library, 1326 W. Cherry Lane. Food Poisoning is one of the most common preventable diseases. You’ll learn about E. coli and other forms of food borne illness, disease symptoms, and how to prevent food poisoning at home and when working with community groups. No charge. Preregister at 377-2107.
Sept 22-24 Residential Landscape Design Short Course will focus on the basics and contemporary issues in landscape design, development, composition and more. Taught by UI professor Stephen Drown, this class costs $179 and is due by Sept. 14. For more information, call Michael Colt at (208)722-6701.
Oct 8
7-9p.m.		 4-H Open House at Ada County Extension. Come learn about 4-H - Head, Heart, Hands & Health - in the Boise area! See what 4-H can do for you! Meet new people, develop leadership skills and more! For more information call Cathy at 377-2107.
Oct 10
9:15 to 4:15		 Rethinking Idaho Landscapes Symposium. Boise Centre-on-the-Grove. Speakers from around the Northwest will talk on a variety of subjects such as, The Layered Garden, In Search of Your Own Garden Paradise and more! For more information call 377-2107 or Michael Colt at 722-6701.
Oct 13
7 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.		 Improving Your Finances. Ada Co. Extension Office. This basic money management class will help you track expenses to control spending, develop a workable spending plan, set financial goals, reduce credit use & establish an emergency fund. Free to those who sign-up for Money 2000, by Sept. 30, $5 for others. For more information call, 377-2107.
Oct 14
10 to 11 a.m.		 Supermarket Strategy. Meridian Library, 1326 W. Cherry Lane. Learn how to maximize your food dollar by developing a workable grocery list, utilizing new shopping skills, and understanding the psychology of the supermarket. No charge. Preregister at 377-2107.
Nov 4
7 to 8:30 p.m.		 Taming the Paper Tiger: Record Keeping. Ada Co. Extension Office. Is organizing your financial records a lot like the weather: do you talk about it but never do anything about it? Learn which records to save and how to set-up paper & computer record keeping systems that put your mind at rest. Cost $5 or no charge for Money 2000 registrants. Preregister at 377-2107.
