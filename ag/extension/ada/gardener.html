Who are Master Gardeners?
Folks within the community who share an interest in gardening and are eager to expand their knowledge of home horticulture.

What is the Master Gardener Program?
An exchange of informal classroom instruction in basic horticulture. This includes 40 hours of classroom training and an additional 40 hours of volunteer hands-on practicum training.

When are classes held?
Master Gardener classes are held from early January through late April (15 weeks, once a week, four hour each session). The hands-on practicum must be completed before October of the same year.

Where are classes held?
Classes are held at the Ada County Extension Office at 5880 Glenwood Ave., Boise, Idaho.

Why are we here?
Because we enjoy gardening and gardeners are really nice folks. We enjoy sharing information. To prove that...

Master Gardeners assist the community by:

  • Teaching gardening classes
  • Answering horticulture questions on the phone and at the office in person between April & October
  • Working on community projects such as the Ronald McDonald House rose garden & landscaping Garden City's City Hall & Library
  • Teaching gardening to children in their classrooms
