4-H
IN ADA COUNTY

4-H is a national youth program for boys girls from ages 5 to 19. 4-H members:

  • Have fun
  • "Learn by doing"
  • Make new friends
  • Help others feel good about themselves
There is a wide choice of unique projects - pet care, food preparation, aerospace, arts and crafts, leadership, and many more - over 100 opportunities. There are other exciting activities such as camps, fairs, Fun Days, judging contests, public speaking and community service.

How to enroll in 4-H:
Any youth between the ages of 5 and 19 years

Enroll anytime
  • The 4-H year in Ada County is Oct 1 - Sept 30
  • Large animal projects have deadlines & dates because of weigh-in & market sales
  • Exhibit at the WIF; enrollment due June 1
Cost to enroll is a publication fee
  • $7.00 for 4-H'ers taking general projects
  • $9.00 for 4-H’ers enrolling in horse projects
What types of projects are available?
  • Citizenship and civic education
  • Community and expressive arts
  • Consumer and family sciences
  • Environmental education & earth sciences
  • Healthy lifestyle education
  • Personal development and leadership
  • Plants and animals
  • Science and technology
Adult volunteers are the "staff" of the 4-H program. These wonderful volunteers, organize, teach and mentor youth. To ensure the program is safe for everyone, adult volunteers must apply and we check references and background. In addition the requirements to be a leader are:
  • Attending 3 leader association meetings/4-H year
  • Pay association dues
  • Attend 2 trainings/4-H year
