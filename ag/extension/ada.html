Frankie L. Marler, Extension Professor and County Chair
M.Ed., Northwest Nazarene College; B.S., University of Idaho

Appointment: 100% Extension (85% Extension Education and 15% Administration). Extension: Extension Educator - 4-H/Youth Development. She coordinates the Ada county 4-H Program and provides leadership for the Canyon County Know Your Government program and for volunteers in the Canyon County 4-H small animal program. Her program emphasis is on volunteer development and management and youth development. Marler develops workshops, training sessions, and materials for volunteer leaders and provides leadership for District II 4-H programs. She collaborates with other youth-serving agencies to improve youth programs and reach new youth audiences. Administration: Marler is County chair of the Ada County Extension Office. The responsibilities of this appointment include office personnel management, liaison with the county commissioners, and coordination of county programs.

Barbara B. Abo, Extension Professor
M.S., Iowa State University; B.S., University of Wisconsin, Stout

Appointment: 100% Extension. Extension: Extension Educator - 4-H/Youth Development. Abo's program emphasis is on 4-H volunteer development and management, 4-H youth programming, and coordination of 4-H programs. For the past five years, she administered a federal grant to provide school-age childcare and education in conjunction with the Boise Public Schools. She developed a school-age child care and education curriculum, trained childcare providers in age-appropriate learning activities, and managed 11 school-age childcare sites. In 1996, the administration of the childcare program passes to the Boise Schools.

Susan M. Bell, Extension Professor
M.S., Southern Illinois University; M.A., Oregon State University; B.A., Quincy College

Appointment: 100% Extension. Extension: Extension Educator - Horticulture. Bell provides leadership for District II horticulture programs and volunteer development. She recruits, trains and manages volunteers in the following programs: Master Gardener, Advanced Master Gardener and Master Composter. She teaches and provides information in the areas of plant selection, propagation, plant culture, plant disease, pest control, water conservation, soil management, horticultural therapy, and yard waste recycling. She develops workshops, training sessions, and materials for classes in cooperation with Boise Parks System, Boise Water Corporation, Boise Community Education, various horticultural businesses and civic organizations. Bell is a Licensed Agricultural Consultant providing services in disease diagnosis, pesticide recommendations, alternative pest controls, and plant and insect identification. She collaborates with a variety of community organizations and businesses to improve plant health and the environment in public parks, golf courses, Christmas tree plantations and private and public landscapes.

Marilyn Cross Bischoff, Extension Professor
M.S., University of Idaho; B.S., Drexel University

Appointment: 100% Extension. Extension: Extension Educator- Family Resource Management. Bischoff teaches and provides information in the areas of personal and family financial management, time management, food safety, food preservation and basic nutrition. She recruits, trains and manages volunteers in the following programs: Master Food Preservers, Advanced Master Food Preservers, and Extension Homemakers. She initiates and/or chairs coalitions to improve family and economic well-being. Examples of these coalitions are the Idaho financial Literacy coalition and the Women's Financial Information Program.

Linda S. Gossett, Extension Assistant Professor
M.P.A., Boise State University; B.A., San Jose State University

Appointment: 100% Extension. Extension: Extension Educator - Extension Nutrition Program. Gossett administers two federally funded educational programs to improve nutrition and money management in families with young children. She hires and teaches ten paraprofessionals. These paraprofessionals then teach current nutrition information, food buying, food safety, resource management, parenting, and other issues impacting low-income families in Ada, Canyon, and Owyhee counties. Gossett supervises paraprofessionals as they teach practical food and nutrition lessons in small groups. She administers the budgets and supervises two secretaries. She collaborates with state and local agencies and the court system to reach target families.
